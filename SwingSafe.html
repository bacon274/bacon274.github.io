SwingSafe

The smart back brace for golfers.

2019 - Group Project with Lidia Dynes-Martinez and Renke Huang

SwingSafe is a smart back brace for golfers that helps prevent lower back injury.

Check out the Kickstarter campaign

Background

Golf is such an important part of many people's lives.

Shockingly golf injuries are more frequent than many contact sports such as rugby and boxing with lower back injury being the most common [1].

SwingSafe aims to change this.

How it works

SwingSafe is a wearable device, worn on the lower back, which when calibrated can alert players as to when they have performed a swing with a high risk of injury to their back. This helps players learn their safe limits and extend their golfing career.

User Testing

The SwingSafe team got to know golfers at Ealing Golf Club in West London. This helped us understand real painpoints and test our assumptions about the product.

Testing a swing while using our product.

Exploring app features.

Their feedback was vital to shaping the product.

We discovered the strong relationship betwen players and Ricky, the Pro in the Pro-Shop. Ricky knew exactly who at they club would benefit from our product and people trusted him. Therefore, we adjusted our business model accordingly to utilise this key channel to reach our core customers.

We also learnt about golf green etiquette, and how discretion and minimal phone usage would have to considered. This led to us changing the feedback system from audible to vibrational.

The Market

Golf is a lucrative sport, demonstrating stable or accelerated growth across all major markets. Euromonitor estimates the global population of golfers at 8OM+ supporting a market that in 2016 was worth over $130 billion.

In the UK, where we hope to focus our efforts initially, there are 11 M+ golfers and a market worth of £43 billion. The population of golfers is increasing at a rate of 1.27% per annum in the UK and finding newfound popularity in Asia.

The proportion of golfers aged 60+ was 25% in 2016 and this demographic is predicted to take 42% of the average increase of golfers by 2020.

Over a third of amateur golfers experience back injuries and this issue is expected to grow as the ageing population shifts.

The opportunity for our product is driven by a surge in the adoption of consumer technology by golfing communities. The number of Golfers that use smartphones is up 47% since 2011. Online sales are up 39% since 2011. The use of technology on the course is undeniable with 64% watching instructional videos. 47% using a launch monitor and 22% using a stat tracking system.
